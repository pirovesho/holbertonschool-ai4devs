# Reflection on AI-Assisted Modernization of WordPress Legacy Code

Working with AI to analyze and plan the modernization of the WordPress codebase provided significant advantages, especially in understanding complex legacy structures. The AI-generated explanations were most helpful in breaking down dense classes like `WP_Query` and the hooks system. These components contain deeply nested logic and multiple responsibilities, making them challenging to comprehend manually. The AI was able to translate technical functions and procedures into clear, plain-English descriptions, highlighting dependencies, potential pitfalls, and structural weaknesses. This clarity accelerated the assessment phase and helped identify which modules posed the highest risk, allowing me to prioritize short-term stabilization and testing.

AI struggled, however, when asked to provide detailed improvement strategies and mitigation plans. While it could identify general issues like “global state reliance” or “inconsistent coding standards,” its suggestions occasionally lacked specific actionable steps or context for practical implementation. For example, the initial notes on performance bottlenecks and plugin security risks were too high-level, requiring additional human reasoning to propose precise refactoring methods or architectural patterns. Similarly, AI sometimes generated overly generic recommendations for code modernization, such as “refactor into classes,” which needed further refinement to align with WordPress’s unique architecture and backward compatibility constraints.

Despite these challenges, AI significantly influenced the modernization strategy. It helped structure the approach into clear phases: short-term stabilization, medium-term incremental refactoring, and long-term architectural improvements. By providing explanations of each complex module, AI informed which components should be targeted first for testing and refactoring. Moreover, AI-assisted risk assessments highlighted potential failure points and informed mitigation strategies, ensuring that modernization efforts would minimize disruption to existing functionality. The structured outputs from AI, such as risks, issues, and recommended improvements, provided a foundation for methodical decision-making and planning.

Several lessons emerged from this exercise. First, AI is most effective as an assistant for understanding and documenting legacy code rather than as a complete decision-maker. Human expertise remains essential for evaluating trade-offs, prioritizing refactors, and tailoring recommendations to project-specific constraints. Second, AI can accelerate planning and reduce cognitive load by generating clear explanations and structured summaries, which is particularly valuable in large, complex codebases. Third, iterative verification is critical: AI outputs should always be reviewed, refined, and supplemented with practical experience to ensure they are actionable. Finally, integrating AI into legacy modernization encourages a systematic approach, allowing developers to move from chaotic codebases toward more maintainable and modern architectures with confidence.

In conclusion, AI proved to be a powerful tool for understanding and documenting the WordPress legacy system, shaping a structured modernization plan, and identifying key risks. However, its outputs required careful human oversight, interpretation, and adaptation to effectively guide practical modernization strategies.
